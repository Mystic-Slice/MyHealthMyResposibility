RECOMENDED ACTIONS

Understanding calorie Balance:

As per the self-assessment section, you have calculated the Calories consumed (A), the required calories for your daily activities (B) and exercise activities (C) (Please refer to the Self-assessment section).
Your total calorie out D = B+C.
As per energy balance it can be balanced in three ways,
  • weight loss
  • weight gain
  • weight Maintenance

Weight Loss

If you are consuming less than required calories (Calorie Deficit), the required energy to meet your energy requirements is received by burning the existing body weight.

Weight Gain

If you are consuming more than D (Calorie Surplus), it turns out as excess body weight and adds to your body weight over a period of time.

Weight Maintenance

If you are consuming the calories to meet your daily needs (both exercise and daily activities), then there will not be any weight change over a period of time. Without a change in the weight, if you want to focus on overall nourishment, you can change the constituents of dietary intake
e.g. reduce the carbs and increase the protein to maintain healthy proportions of nutrients required for nourishment.

In the Calories Balance, you can plan your weight loss program by

  1. Reducing the calorie intake over a period of time (A)
  2. Increasing the exercise duration and burning more calories ( C )

Planning weight loss:

a) Reducing the calorie intake (A)
Weight loss target: 5 kg
Weight loss target period: 90 days
Target Calorie loss per day: 5 * 7500/90 = 417 K Cal.

Planning your intake:
You need to reduce daily calorie intake by 417, (less than the required calories), in this example: (2420-417) = 2003 Calories. Limit your intake to 2003 Calories.
The person by reducing the calorie intake by 417 calories for a period of 90 days, he (or she) is likely to lose 5 kg of weight.

b) Increasing the exercise calorie expenditure (C )
If the person also adds the further loss of calories through exercises
e.g. 400 Calories daily for a period of 90 days, he is likely to lose another 5 Kg. (400 * 90 days / 7500)
(For weight gain, similar calculations can be done, by increasing the daily calorie intake (A), over target period, follow up plan to gain weight.)
